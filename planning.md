# Provenance Guard: System Specification

## 1. Detection Signals
The system uses two distinct signals to classify content:
1.  **Groq LLM (`llama-3.3-70b-versatile`)**: This signal captures semantic and stylistic coherence holistically. It measures whether the text reads as human or AI-generated by asking the model to assess the text. 
    *   **Output**: A score between 0.0 and 1.0 (where 1.0 means highly likely AI-generated).
    *   **Blind Spot**: It may incorrectly flag highly formal or formulaic human writing (e.g., legal texts or technical documentation) as AI.
2.  **Stylometric Heuristics**: This signal measures structural and statistical properties of the text that tend to differ between human and AI writing. We compute two specific metrics: *Sentence length variance* and *Type-token ratio (vocabulary diversity)*.
    *   **Output**: A normalized score between 0.0 and 1.0 (where 1.0 means highly uniform, consistent with AI).
    *   **Blind Spot**: It may incorrectly flag human writing that has been heavily edited for uniformity or brevity.

**Combination Logic**: The two signal scores will be averaged to produce a single, unified confidence score between 0.0 and 1.0.

## 2. Uncertainty Representation
A confidence score of 0.6 means the system detects some AI-like characteristics (e.g., low vocabulary diversity or high stylistic uniformity) but lacks the definitive markers of either pure AI generation or completely natural human writing. It represents genuine uncertainty.

We map the combined raw signal output to the following calibrated thresholds:
*   **Likely AI-generated**: Score > 0.70
*   **Uncertain**: Score 0.30 - 0.70
*   **Likely human-written**: Score < 0.30

## 3. Transparency Label Design
The transparency label will be displayed directly to the end-user on the platform. The exact text variants are:

| Confidence Score Range | Transparency Label Text |
| :--- | :--- |
| **> 0.70** | "Likely AI-generated" |
| **0.30 - 0.70** | "Uncertain origin" |
| **< 0.30** | "Likely human-written" |

## 4. Appeals Workflow
*   **Who can submit?**: Any creator who submits content and receives a classification they believe is inaccurate.
*   **What information is provided?**: The creator must provide the original `content_id` and a `creator_reasoning` text field explaining why the classification is incorrect (e.g., explaining their unique writing style or demographic context).
*   **System Action**: The system logs the appeal alongside the original classification decision in the audit log and updates the content's status to `"under_review"`. It then returns a success response to the user.
*   **Human Reviewer View**: A human reviewer opening the appeal queue will see the original text, the individual signal scores, the combined confidence score, the transparency label that was issued, and the creator's written reasoning.

## 5. Anticipated Edge Cases
The system is likely to handle the following scenarios poorly:
1.  **Highly repetitive, stylized poetry**: A poem with heavy use of repetition and simple vocabulary might score very high on the stylometric heuristic (due to low sentence length variance and low type-token ratio), causing a false positive for AI generation.
2.  **Lightly edited AI output**: If a creator uses an LLM to generate a first draft but heavily edits the structure, adding slang or irregular sentence lengths, the LLM signal might detect semantic AI traces, while the stylometric signal might look highly human. This will likely result in an "Uncertain origin" label, frustrating a user who feels they did significant manual work.

## Architecture

**Narrative:**
When a piece of text is submitted via `POST /submit`, it is processed through two signals: the Groq LLM (Signal 1) and Stylometric Heuristics (Signal 2). Both signals return independent scores, which are averaged into a single confidence score. This score is then mapped to a transparency label ("Likely AI-generated", "Uncertain origin", or "Likely human-written"). The final decision, along with all component scores, is saved to an SQLite Audit Log before being returned to the user.
If a creator contests the label via `POST /appeal`, the system locates the submission in the Audit Log, updates its status to "under_review", and appends the creator's reasoning to the log.
